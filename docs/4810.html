Palmer Street, Cambridge lighting sculpture • concept details for review • v. 4.3 [page 1 of 7] RMS, Inc. 3.26.18 PROJECT Sculptural lighting for Palmer Street - inspired by the play of free-form contour line drawing. Continuous lines of light trace gentle loops, improvised curves and swirls, supported within a net matrix. This installation is created using a new type of full color RGB chip LED light source embedded in a ﬂexible weatherproof silicone extrusion. Color adjustments within individual lines may be made manually with a wireless remote. Color variations may be selected to mark special occasions, or shift seasonally. The diffused LED sources of the installation produce enough lumens to provide an overspill of color light creating a glow against adjacent brick walls. The artwork is centrally positioned at the dark mid-point of Palmer Street, drawing the eye, and the viewer, in from both Church and Brattle Streets. At this time no automatic programming is planned. One can imagine a system with complicated sequences and patterns, driven by micro- processor controls, and at a much greater cost for ﬁxtures, electronics and software. However, the intent of this project is to engage the space with light in a surprising yet gentle and calm way, rather than with ﬂashy whiz- bang graphics. The hope is for the artwork to be discovered as a delightful moment within a drab alleyway. Sometimes as foreground, at other moments becoming part of the background. Something to be enjoyed and lived with on a daily basis.
