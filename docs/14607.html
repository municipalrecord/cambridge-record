Letter from former City Clerk, D. Margaret Drury, thanking the City Council on the resolution regarding her retirement from the Board of Cambridge Redevelopment Authority

To The Honorable, the City Council: Dear Members of the City Council: I am writing to thank you for the resolution regarding my retirement from the board of the Cambridge Redevelopment Authority. I am very proud of the work that was done by the entire staff, the entire board, Executive Director Tom Evans, Chair of the Board Kathleen Born, Vice Chair Conrad Crawford, Treasurer Christopher Bator, and I, the Assistant Secretary. I hope you all will take the opportunity to see the many projects that the CRA has been involved in, especially the Foundry and the rehabilitation of 99 Bishop Allen Drive. Equally as important is the strong mission for the advancement of the public good in the City of Cambridge. I am also very proud of my work for the City of Cambridge, and I am grateful to have had the opportunity to take part in the very special place that it is. Again, I thank you. Sincerely, manganes 017 D. Margaret Drury
