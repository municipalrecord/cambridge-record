689 Massachusetts Ave, (617) 349 - 6155 Cambridge, MA 02139 www.cambridgema.gov/PRAB Minutes from the Meeting of December 10, 2025 Board Members Present: Chair Gina LaRoche, Maria Arlotto, Alexandra Fallon City Staﬀ present: Executive Secretary Carolina Almonte Cambridge Police Department (CPD) Staﬀ Present: Director of Professional Standards & Training James Mulcahy, Sergeant Robert Ciriello, Sergeant David Barkhouse Chair LaRoche called the meeting to order at 6:07 p.m. Public Comment Ryan Welsh, Linda Burns, Laurel Shugart, and Catiana JP joined as a members of the public. Review and Approval of Minutes On a motion from Board Member Arlotto, seconded by Board Member Fallon, the minutes from November 12, 2025, were approved unanimously. Presentation and Conversation with Jackson Price, Director of Community Engagement Mr. Price provided an overview of how Cambridge approaches equitable and inclusive community engagement. He explained that more than ﬁfty engagement eﬀorts occur citywide each year and his oﬃce supports staﬀ through toolkits, best practices, and a Civic Outreach Squad focused on equitable outreach. Mr. Price outlined core principles for eﬀective engagement, including clearly identifying the intended audience, aligning plans with available timelines and budgets, clarifying whether the goal is outreach or genuine engagement, selecting appropriate communication methods, and establishing metrics to evaluate success. He emphasized the importance of closing the loop with community members, so they understand how their input shaped decisions. Mr. Price also recommended that PRAB partner with CPD’s Director of Community Outreach to align eﬀorts and expand reach. He closed by oﬀering to answer questions from the Board. Report from CPD Professional Standards, Training and Certiﬁcation Unit Director Mulcahy Director Mulcahy shared that the PSU team attended the CALEO Conference in Florida, where they were recognized with an award for their work. He noted that the Police Academy is scheduled to begin in the new year.

Bodyworn Camera Training - Deputy Superintendent Vellucci and Mike Horgan Deputy Superintendent Vellucci and Mr. Horgan summarized CPD’s Body Worn Camera policy, explaining when oﬃcers are required to activate cameras, including dispatched calls for service, traﬃc stops, investigative stops, arrests, use of force incidents, pursuits, and other civilian interactions for law enforcement purposes. They noted that activation is required even in locations where there may be a reasonable expectation of privacy and emphasized that the policy is intended to enhance transparency, accountability, and public trust.
